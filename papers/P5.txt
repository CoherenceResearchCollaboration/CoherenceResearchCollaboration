Theorem: the ghost transition in the forbidden zone

Theorem 4 (The golden ratio transition is forbidden). The energy ΔE = IE/φ falls strictly inside the forbidden zone [IE/4, 3IE/4]. This energy is what the transition n = 1 → n = φ would carry if φ were a positive integer. Because φ is irrational, the transition does not exist. The gap is the spectroscopic record of the attractor being unreachable.

Proof. For a hydrogenic transition from initial state n_(i) to final state n_(f) = 1:
$$\Delta E(n_i) = E_0\!\left(1 - \frac{1}{n_i^2}\right).$$
At n_(i) = φ, using φ² = φ + 1:
$$\frac{1}{\varphi^2} = \frac{1}{\varphi+1} = 2 - \varphi,$$
where the last equality uses 1/φ² + φ = 2. Therefore:
$$\Delta E(\varphi)
  = E_0\!\left(1-(2-\varphi)\right)
  = E_0(\varphi-1)
  = \frac{E_0}{\varphi},$$
where the last step uses φ − 1 = 1/φ. Numerically: IE/φ = 8404.3 meV = 0.61803 IE. We must show 1/4 < 1/φ < 3/4:
$$\frac{1}{\varphi}
  = \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2} \approx 0.61803,$$
and 0.25 < 0.618 < 0.75 is an exact statement about $(\sqrt{5}-1)/2$. ◻

The Minkowski signature

The energy space ℳ is equipped with the metric
ds² = dℰ_(H)² − dℰ_(E)²,
which has Minkowski, not Euclidean, signature. This is not borrowed from special relativity. It follows from the physical character of the two modes established in Proposition 1, and ultimately from the primitive identification of ε₀ and μ₀ established in (Heaton 2026c), Section 1.

The E-mode field lines are meridional: they terminate on charges, defining the current spatial locus [1] of the field configuration at quantum number n. The coordinate ℰ_(E) is the energy of that locus: determinate, positioned, and non-directed — it can be read at any n without reference to what preceded it.

The H-mode field lines are latitudinal: they circuit the source pole p without terminating, accumulating one unit of holonomy per winding and measuring the relational depth of the process from its origin. The coordinate ℰ_(H) is the energy of that accumulated depth: causally ordered, because each successive Lyman transition represents the process returning to ground after one further winding around p.

These two coordinates are not symmetric. The energy of a locus can be compared without causal constraint — ℰ_(E) at n = 3 and ℰ_(E) at n = 5 are freely interchangeable as spatial configurations. The energy of an accumulated process cannot — ℰ_(H) is ordered by the winding depth that produced it. One coordinate is free; the other is directed. The natural invariant of a space with one free and one directed coordinate is their difference, not their sum: summing them symmetrically would collapse the causal ordering. This fixes the signature as (−,+).

The unit coefficients are fixed by a separate consideration. Both ℰ_(E) and ℰ_(H) are energies measured in identical units, and Proposition 1 establishes the E- and H-modes as orthogonal sectors of equal geometric standing. The unique indefinite metric with equal-weight coordinates and no free dimensionless ratio is ds² = dℰ_(H)² − dℰ_(E)², determined by signature and symmetry before any spectroscopic data enters.
